About Me

I'm a Computational Linguist and AI Engineer with a passion for developing scalable AI applications and exploring the intersections of language, technology, and artificial intelligence.

Who I Am

With a Ph.D. in Computational Linguistics from the University of Hyderabad, I specialize in developing AI applications with a focus on NLP, transformer architectures, and Gen AI. My expertise spans across Python, Flask, and cloud deployments.

I'm passionate about integrating cutting-edge LLMs (such as GPT, Llama) and data pipelines to revolutionize recruitment and data extraction. My research interests include verb semantics, dependency structures, and Bengali language computing.

Throughout my career, I've worked on various projects including sentiment analysis models, PDF processing applications, and YouTube video recommendation systems. I enjoy tackling complex problems and creating innovative solutions using AI and linguistic knowledge.

Academic Publications

Evaluating YouTube Videos via Sentiment Analysis: A Case Study in Bangla-English Code-mixed Content

Kar, A. and Jana, A. (2023)

Lecture Notes in Networks and Systems. Springer

This paper presents a novel approach to evaluate Bangla YouTube videos through sentiment analysis of code-mixed comments. We demonstrate a practical application of our fine-tuned sentiment analysis model, achieving 93% accuracy in determining viewer response polarity.

Use of Ontology in Bengali Verb Frame and Its Importance in Verb Sense Disambiguation

Kar, A. (2021)

Jadavpur Journal of Language and Linguistics, Vol 4, Pages 161-172

This research examines the application of ontological frameworks to Bengali verb semantics, proposing a novel approach to verb sense disambiguation. The paper establishes connections between semantic roles, ontological categories, and verb meanings in Bengali language processing.
